Origin · The Dragon's Head

Designer-led, Yaowarat-grown.

Two interior designers and a food stylist walked into a Chalermburi shophouse in July 2018 and re-imagined dim sum as the daylight ritual it always wanted to be.

The name Lhong Tou — 龙头, Teochew for dragon's head — names the corner the cafe sits on: the literal start of Yaowarat Road, where the dragon of Bangkok's Chinatown begins its body. Inside, a double-decker climbing-frame seating system folds two stories of breakfast crowd into one shophouse footprint. Outside, neon-handpainted signage and a heavy timber door.
