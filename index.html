№ 02 · 起源 · The Origin

龙头之首 At the dragon's head of Yaowarat.

Lhong Tou (龙头) means "dragon's head" in Teochew, the Chinese dialect of Bangkok's Chinatown — and the cafe earned that name because it sits at the Chalermburi intersection, the literal head of Yaowarat Road. Walk down the rest of the street and you're inside the dragon.

The cafe opened in July 2018 — a project by the team behind Sukhumvit steakhouse Meat Bar 31. Two interior designers and a food stylist. The shophouse was narrow, so they bolted dark wood planks to industrial scaffolding pipes and stacked the tables vertically. Some seats sit a metre above the floor and you climb a small ladder to get there.

"The cafe amplifies Yaowarat's neon signage with its own Chinese lettering that screams in electric red and green at passersby." — BK Magazine · BK Staff · Jul 2018

The menu is a hipster reworking of Chinatown breakfast — crisp-shelled salted-egg lava buns at ฿29, Lhong Tou shu mai in a sweet-savoury sauce, a Chinese breakfast set built from ingredients hand-picked from neighbouring shops, and a Thai milk tea topped with kanom toob tub, the peanut-sesame snack from the street outside.

Look up while you queue and you'll see traditional ornaments — peacock, swallow, bird's nest — set against the neon. The whole place reads like a Teochew-shophouse blueprint a designer pinned to a Pinterest board and built in three dimensions.
